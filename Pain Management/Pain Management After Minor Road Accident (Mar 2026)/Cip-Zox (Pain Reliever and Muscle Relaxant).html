Cip-Zox is a combination medication primarily used to relieve pain, inflammation, and stiffness caused by muscle spasms and musculoskeletal injuries. It is manufactured by Cipla Ltd.. 
Composition 
Each tablet typically contains three active ingredients: 
  • Chlorzoxazone (500 mg): A muscle relaxant that acts on the brain and spinal cord to relieve stiffness.
  • Diclofenac Sodium (50 mg): A non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that reduces pain and swelling.
  • Paracetamol (325 mg): An analgesic and antipyretic that reduces pain and fever. 
Key Uses 
  • Muscle Spasms: Relief from painful muscle contractions and stiffness.
  • Injuries: Treatment for strains, sprains, and sports-related muscle injuries.
  • Arthritis & Spondylitis: Management of pain and inflammation in joints and bones.
  • Post-Surgical Recovery: Relieving pain and swelling following operations. 
Important Safety Information 
  • Administration: It is highly recommended to take this medicine with food or milk to prevent stomach upset or irritation.
  • Alcohol: Avoid alcohol as it increases the risk of liver damage and stomach bleeding.
  • Driving: May cause dizziness or drowsiness; do not drive or operate machinery until you know how it affects you.
  • Pregnancy & Breastfeeding: Generally considered unsafe; consult a doctor before use.
  • Contraindications: Not recommended for individuals with severe heart, liver, or kidney disease, or a history of stomach ulcers. 
Common Side Effects 
  • Nausea and vomiting.
  • Stomach pain or indigestion.
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness.
  • Drowsiness or fatigue.
  • Note: Chlorzoxazone may cause a harmless orange or reddish tint to urine.
